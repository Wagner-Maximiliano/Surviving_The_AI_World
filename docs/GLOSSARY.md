# Glossary

## Algorithm
A step-by-step procedure that a computer follows to solve a problem or complete a task. Think of it like a recipe with exact instructions that anyone could follow to get the same result every time.

## Artificial Intelligence
Technology that enables machines to perform tasks that typically require human thinking, such as learning from experience, recognizing patterns, or making decisions. AI ranges from simple automated systems to advanced models that can generate text or images.

## Automation
The use of technology to handle tasks without human intervention. By automating repetitive work, organizations can save time and reduce errors while freeing people to focus on more complex problems.

## Bias (AI)
Systematic errors or unfair patterns that can emerge when AI systems are trained on data that doesn't represent everyone equally. An AI trained mostly on data from one demographic may make worse decisions for other groups, reflecting and amplifying real-world inequalities.

## Data
The raw information—numbers, text, images, or other formats—that computers use to learn patterns and make decisions. The quality and size of data directly impact how well AI systems can work.

## Large Language Model
A type of AI trained on vast amounts of text to understand and generate human language. These models power many chatbots and writing assistants that can answer questions and produce fluent text on almost any topic.

## Machine Learning
The capability of AI systems to improve their performance by learning from examples rather than being explicitly programmed for each task. Instead of writing rules by hand, machine learning systems find patterns in data and adjust their behavior accordingly.

## Neural Network
A computational structure inspired by how biological brains work, using interconnected layers of simple processing units. Neural networks are especially good at recognizing patterns in images, text, and other complex data.

## Parameter
A numerical setting or weight inside an AI model that gets adjusted during training to improve performance. Modern large language models contain billions of parameters that are fine-tuned to generate accurate and helpful responses.

## Training Data
The examples and information used to teach an AI system how to perform a task. If the training data is biased, incomplete, or poor quality, the AI system will likely produce biased or unreliable results.
